Study Debrief

This experiment investigated the "Ecological Valence Theory" of color preferences, introduced by Palmer & Schloss (2010). Their research asked a fundamental question: Why do we like the colours we like? Their theory proposes a remarkably simple explanation: we like colours in proportion to how much they remind us of positive things. For example, if blue reminds you of clear skies and clean water (positive associations), you're likely to rate blue colours highly. Using this data, they calculated a "Weighted Affective Valence Estimate" (WAVE) for each color - essentially measuring how positive the associations were for each colour. Remarkably, these WAVE scores explained over 80% of the variance in colour preferences.

The researchers tested this by having three groups of participants:

  • Group 1 rated how much they liked different colors (similar to what you just did)

  • Group 2 listed objects they associated with each colour

  • Group 3 rated how positive or negative those objects were


Your results show your individual colour preferences across different dimensions of colour HSL (hue, saturation, and lightness). These preferences might reflect your personal experiences and associations with different colours - the positive or negative memories, objects, and experiences you connect with each colour.
